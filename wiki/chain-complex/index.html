Introduction

Definitions

A chain complex is a sequence of abelian groups \( C_n \) (one for each \( n\in\mathbb{Z} \)) together with maps \( d_n:C_n\to C_{n-1} \) called *differentials* such that \( d_nd_{n+1}=0 \) for every \( n\in\mathbb{Z} \).

We define the group of n-cycles \(Z_n(C_\bullet) := \ker(d_n:C_n\to C_{n-1})\), and we define the *group of n-boundaries* \(B_n(C_\bullet) := {\rm im}(d_{n+1}:C_{n+1}\to C_n)\).

Since \(d^2=0\), \(B_n\subset Z_n\). This permits us to define the n-homology group to be the quotient

\[ H_n(C_\bullet) := \frac{Z_n(C_\bullet)}{B_n(C_\bullet)} = \frac{{\rm im}(d:C_{n+1}\to C_n)}{\ker(d_n:C_n\to C_{n-1})}. \]
