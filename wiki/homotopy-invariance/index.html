Introduction

Homotopy invariance is a key theorem from algebraic topology. It says that if two maps are homotopic then they induce the same singular homology homomorphism. The prerequisite notions for this theorem are singular homology and the prism operator.

Statement and Proof

Theorem: Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be topological spaces. If \(f,g:X\to Y\) are homotopic, then their induced singular homology homomorphisms are equal:

\[ f_* = g_* : H_n(X) \to H_n(Y). \]

Proof: Let \(H:X\times I\to Y\) be the homotopy between \(f\) and \(g\). Define \(P:C_n(X)\to C_{n+1}(Y)\) to be the prism operator. Then since \(H\circ(\sigma\times{\rm id})|_{\Delta\times\{1\}}=g\circ\sigma=[w_0,\cdots,w_n]\) and \(H\circ(\sigma\times{\rm id})|_{\Delta\times\{0\}}=f\circ\sigma=[v_0,\cdots,v_n]\), we have

\[ \partial P(\sigma) = g_*(\sigma) - f_*(\sigma) - P(\partial\sigma). \]

Therefore for if \(\sigma\in Z_n(X)\) then \(\partial\sigma=0\) and so

\[ g_*(\sigma) - f_*(\sigma) = \partial P(\sigma), \]

i.e. \( g_*(\sigma) \) and \( f_*(\sigma) \) differ by a boundary, and so passing to the quotient \( H_n(X) = Z_n(X)/B_n(X) \) we have \( g_*(\sigma) = f_*(\sigma) \).

QED.

Consequences

Corollary: If \(X\) and \(Y\) are homotopy equivalent then \(H_n(X)\cong H_n(Y)\) for all \(n\).

Proof: \(X\) and \(Y\) being homotopy equivalent means exactly that there are maps \(f:X\to Y\) and \(g:Y\to X\) such that \(gf\simeq{\rm id}X\) and \(fg\simeq{\rm id}Y\). Now, we have the induced homomorphisms \(f*:H_n(X)\to H_n(Y)\) and \(g*:H_n(Y)\to H_n(X)\).

By homotopy equivalence (the above theorem), we have that

\[g_*f_* = (gf)_* = ({\rm id}_X)_* = {\rm id}_{H_n(X)}\] \[f_*g_* = (fg)_* = ({\rm id}_Y)_* = {\rm id}_{H_n(Y)}.\]

So \(f_*\) and \(g_*\) are inverses of each other, and so they are both group isomorphisms. So \(H_n(X)\cong H_n(Y)\).

QED.

Corollary: If \(X\) is contractible, then

\[ H_n(X) = \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} & n=0 \\
0 & n>0. \end{cases} \]

Proof: \(X\) being contractible means exactly that \(X\) is homotopy equivalent to a point, and by the above corollary their homology groups are equal, which yields the desired result.

QED.
