Introduction

Let \(X\) be a topological space and let \(A\subset X\). Intuitively, the relative homology of \(A\) in \(X\) is the “homology where we don’t care about what happens in A”.

Definitions

Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(A\subset X\). Then the group of \(n\)-cycles in \(A\) is a subgroup of the group of \(n\)-cycles in \(X\). Therefore the boundary map \(\partial:C_n(X)\to C_{n-1}(X)\) descends to a map on the quotients

\[ \partial:C_n(X)/C_n(A)\to C_{n-1}(X)/C_{n-1}(A). \]

We define \( C_n(X,A):=C_n(X)/C_n(A) \). This gives us a chain complex called the relative chain complex of the pair \((X,A)\), and so we can pass to homology via the standard algebraic procedure

\[ H_n(X,A) := H_n(C_\bullet(X,A)), \]

giving the relative homology of the pair \((X,A)\), which we recall is just the quotient \( {\rm im\,}{\partial_{n+1}}/\ker{\partial_n} \).
